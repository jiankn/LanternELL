Sentence Frames

1

I need to locate reliable sources for my research on _______________.

2

Dialogue Strips

Cut along the dotted lines. Practice with a partner.

Students discussing a research project
A
I need to locate reliable sources for my research on climate change.

Necesito localizar fuentes confiables para mi investigación sobre el cambio climático.

B
When evaluating a source, I consider its authority, accuracy, and purpose. That might help you!

Al evaluar una fuente, considero su autoridad, precisión y propósito. ¡Eso podría ayudarte!

Students seeking help with citation
A
I am having difficulty understanding how to properly format a citation for a website.

Estoy teniendo dificultades para entender cómo formatear correctamente una cita para un sitio web.

B
To avoid plagiarism, I must always cite the original source of information. Let's look at the example in our textbook together.

Para evitar el plagio, siempre debo citar la fuente original de información. Veamos el ejemplo en nuestro libro de texto juntos.

Students sharing their research questions and findings
A
My research question is 'How does deforestation impact biodiversity?', and I hope to find evidence to support my claim.

Mi pregunta de investigación es '¿Cómo afecta la deforestación a la biodiversidad?', y espero encontrar evidencia que apoye mi afirmación.

B
The evidence from this source helps to strengthen my argument about the Amazon rainforest. Maybe you can find something similar.

La evidencia de esta fuente ayuda a fortalecer mi argumento sobre la selva amazónica. Quizás puedas encontrar algo similar.

Students clarifying research terms
A
Can you explain the difference between a primary and secondary source?

¿Puedes explicar la diferencia entre una fuente primaria y una secundaria?

B
I will paraphrase this information in my own words to demonstrate understanding, but broadly, primary sources are original, and secondary sources analyze those originals.

Parafrasearé esta información con mis propias palabras para demostrar comprensión, pero en general, las fuentes primarias son originales y las secundarias analizan esos originales.

LanternELLTeacher Notes📄 Printable Pack

Teacher Notes

🎯 Objective

Students will be able to use academic language frames to discuss research processes, source evaluation, and citation practices in English.

💡 Suggested Use

Introduce frames one by one, modeling pronunciation and usage. Practice in pairs or small groups through structured dialogues. Use speaking prompts for whole-class discussion or individual assessment. Worksheets can be used for independent practice or homework. Encourage students to keep a 'Research & Citation' vocabulary journal.

📦 Materials Needed

  • Whiteboard or projector
  • Markers/pens
  • Copies of sentence frames, dialogue strips, speaking prompts, and worksheets
  • Index cards or small whiteboards for quick responses
  • Access to computers/internet for example research (optional)

🔀 Differentiation Tips

  • **For beginning ELLs:** Provide sentence starters or word banks for blanks. Allow students to use L1 for initial brainstorming. Pair with a more proficient English speaker.
  • **For intermediate ELLs:** Encourage elaboration on responses. Ask 'why' and 'how' questions. Challenge them to use multiple frames in one response.
  • **For advanced ELLs:** Have them create their own dialogue strips or speaking prompts. Encourage debate or critical analysis using the frames.
