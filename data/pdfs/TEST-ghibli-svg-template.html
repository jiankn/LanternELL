LanternELLTeacher Notes📎 Sample

Teacher Notes

🎯 Objective

Students will be able to identify and name at least 15 common classroom objects in English and Spanish. Students will use new vocabulary in simple sentences.

💡 Suggested Use

Introduce vocabulary using flashcards and realia. Use the mini-book for shared reading and repeated practice. Worksheets can be completed independently or in small groups. Encourage students to point to and name objects in the classroom.

📦 Materials Needed

  • Flashcards of classroom objects (included vocabulary)
  • Real classroom objects (pencil, book, chair, etc.)
  • Crayons, markers, or colored pencils
  • Pencils for tracing and writing
  • Glue sticks for optional extension activities (e.g., cut and paste)

🔀 Differentiation Tips

  • **Support:** Provide sentence frames (e.g., 'It's a ___.', 'I see a ___ .'), use TPR (Total Physical Response) by having students touch or point to objects, pair students with a stronger English speaker, use visuals extensively.
  • **Challenge:** Ask students to describe objects (e.g., 'What color is the pencil?'), use objects in a sentence, identify objects by their function (e.g., 'What do we use to cut paper?'), create their own mini-book page.
