Academic Discussion Frames for Grades 3-5

This pack provides functional sentence frames to help newcomer ELL students in grades 3-5 participate in academic discussions, covering expressing opinions, agreeing, disagreeing, clarifying, and summarizing.

TopicAcademic Discussion
Age Band3-5
Pack Typesentence frames
LanguageEN-ES

✨ What's Inside

  • 2 worksheets
  • 12 sentence frames
  • 4 dialogue strips
  • 4 speaking prompts
  • Teacher notes & answer key

📖 How to Use

Introduce 2-3 frames at a time. Model their use with a partner or whole class. Practice in pairs or small groups using the dialogue strips and speaking prompts. Encourage students to use the frames during regular classroom discussions across subjects. The worksheets can be used for independent practice or assessment.

Sentence Frames

1

I think that [idea], because [reason].

Creo que [idea], porque [razón].

2

In my opinion, [topic] is important, and I believe [reason].

En mi opinión, [tema] es importante, y creo que [razón].

3

I agree with [person's name] because [reason], and I also think [additional idea].

Estoy de acuerdo con [nombre de la persona] porque [razón], y también pienso que [idea adicional].

4

I disagree with [person's name] because [reason], but I understand [their point of view].

No estoy de acuerdo con [nombre de la persona] porque [razón], pero entiendo [su punto de vista].

5

Could you please explain [your idea] again, so I can understand it better?

¿Podrías explicar [tu idea] otra vez, por favor, para que pueda entenderla mejor?

6

Another point to consider is [new idea], and it relates to [previous idea].

Otro punto a considerar es [nueva idea], y se relaciona con [idea anterior].

Sentence Frames

1

To clarify, are you saying that [rephrase idea]?

Para aclarar, ¿estás diciendo que [reformular idea]?

2

3

This reminds me of [personal experience/other topic], and it helps me understand [concept].

Esto me recuerda a [experiencia personal/otro tema], y me ayuda a entender [concepto].

5

So, to summarize, we discussed [main point 1] and [main point 2].

Entonces, para resumir, discutimos [punto principal 1] y [punto principal 2].

6

Dialogue Strips

Cut along the dotted lines. Practice with a partner.

Students discussing a book character.
A
I think that the main character was brave, because she helped her friend.

Creo que la personaje principal fue valiente, porque ayudó a su amiga.

B
I agree with you because she faced her fears, and I also think she was very kind.

Estoy de acuerdo contigo porque ella enfrentó sus miedos, y también pienso que fue muy amable.

Students discussing a science experiment with plants.
A
In my opinion, the plant needs more water, and I believe it will grow faster.

En mi opinión, la planta necesita más agua, y creo que crecerá más rápido.

B
I disagree with you because too much water can hurt it, but I understand why you think that.

No estoy de acuerdo contigo porque demasiada agua puede dañarla, pero entiendo por qué piensas eso.

Students discussing a historical exploration.
A
I noticed that the explorers brought many tools, which makes me think they were prepared.

Me di cuenta de que los exploradores trajeron muchas herramientas, lo que me hace pensar que estaban preparados.

B
Another point to consider is that they also had maps, and it relates to being prepared for the journey.

Otro punto a considerar es que también tenían mapas, y se relaciona con estar preparados para el viaje.

Students clarifying instructions for an activity.
A
Could you please explain the last step again, so I can understand it better?

¿Podrías explicar el último paso otra vez, por favor, para que pueda entenderlo mejor?

B
To clarify, are you saying that we mix the two liquids after heating them?

Para aclarar, ¿estás diciendo que mezclamos los dos líquidos después de calentarlos?

LanternELLSpeaking Prompts 1📄 Printable Pack

Speaking Prompt Cards

Prompt 1

What is the most important rule in our classroom, and why do you think so?

¿Cuál es la regla más importante en nuestra aula, y por qué piensas eso?

Prompt 2

Look at these two pictures of different animals. Which animal would make a better pet, and why?

Mira estas dos imágenes de diferentes animales. ¿Qué animal sería una mejor mascota, y por qué?

Prompt 3

We just read a story about sharing. What is one way to be a good friend, and how does it help others?

Acabamos de leer una historia sobre compartir. ¿Cuál es una forma de ser un buen amigo, y cómo ayuda a los demás?

Prompt 4

Imagine we are planning a class trip. What is one place you would like to visit, and why?

Imagina que estamos planeando una excursión de clase. ¿Qué lugar te gustaría visitar, y por qué?

LanternELLTeacher Notes📄 Printable Pack

Teacher Notes

🎯 Objective

Students will be able to use functional sentence frames to express opinions, agree, disagree, ask for clarification, and summarize key points in academic discussions.

💡 Suggested Use

Introduce 2-3 frames at a time. Model their use with a partner or whole class. Practice in pairs or small groups using the dialogue strips and speaking prompts. Encourage students to use the frames during regular classroom discussions across subjects. The worksheets can be used for independent practice or assessment.

📦 Materials Needed

  • Whiteboard or chart paper
  • Markers
  • Printed sentence frames (laminated for durability)
  • Printed dialogue strips
  • Printed speaking prompt cards
  • Worksheets
  • A relevant text, image, or topic for discussion (e.g., a short story, a science concept, a current event suitable for grade level)

🔀 Differentiation Tips

  • **For struggling learners:** Provide visual cues for each frame. Allow students to use sentence starters and fill in single words. Pair them with a more proficient peer. Practice choral repetition of frames.
  • **For advanced learners:** Encourage them to combine frames or extend their responses with more complex reasoning. Ask them to lead small group discussions or create their own discussion questions using the frames.
