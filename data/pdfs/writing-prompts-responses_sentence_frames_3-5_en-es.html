Dialogue Strips

Cut along the dotted lines. Practice with a partner.

Students discussing a descriptive writing prompt.
A
The writing prompt asks me to describe my favorite animal, so I will think about its colors.

El indicador de escritura me pide que describa mi animal favorito, así que pensaré en sus colores.

B
That's a good idea! I need to write about my pet, and I will include details like its fur.

¡Esa es una buena idea! Necesito escribir sobre mi mascota, e incluiré detalles como su pelaje.

Students planning a narrative story.
A
My main idea for this story is a magical adventure, and I will start by introducing the hero.

Mi idea principal para esta historia es una aventura mágica, y comenzaré por presentar al héroe.

B
Cool! I will organize my writing by first describing the setting, then the problem, and finally the solution.

¡Genial! Organizaré mi escritura primero describiendo el escenario, luego el problema, y finalmente la solución.

Students discussing how to approach an explanatory prompt.
A
When I read the prompt, I understand that I should explain a process, so I will make sure to use transition words.

Cuando leo el indicador, entiendo que debo explicar un proceso, así que me aseguraré de usar palabras de transición.

B
Good point. I can use descriptive words such as 'sparkling' and 'gigantic' to make my writing better.

Buen punto. Puedo usar palabras descriptivas como 'brillante' y 'gigantesco' para mejorar mi escritura.

Students giving peer feedback on a story.
A
My partner's writing is interesting because they described a mysterious forest, and I liked how they used suspense.

La escritura de mi compañero es interesante porque describieron un bosque misterioso, y me gustó cómo usaron el suspenso.

B
I agree! I want to ask my partner about the monster, because I am curious about its powers in their story.

¡Estoy de acuerdo! Quiero preguntarle a mi compañero sobre el monstruo, porque tengo curiosidad sobre sus poderes en su historia.

Teacher Notes

🎯 Objective

Students will use sentence frames to plan, write, and respond to writing prompts, enhancing their ability to communicate ideas in written and spoken English.

💡 Suggested Use

Introduce frames one by one. Model usage with examples relevant to the students' lives. Practice in pairs or small groups during pre-writing, drafting, and peer review stages. Integrate these frames with existing writing curriculum to support ELL students in various writing tasks.

📦 Materials Needed

  • Whiteboard/projector
  • Markers
  • Sentence frame printouts
  • Writing paper
  • Pencils
  • Colored pencils for visual cues
  • Peer review checklists (optional)

🔀 Differentiation Tips

  • Support: Provide word banks for fill-in-the-blank activities. Pre-teach key vocabulary before writing tasks. Allow students to draw their ideas before writing. Pair newcomer students with stronger English speakers for peer activities.
  • Challenge: Encourage students to extend the frames with more complex ideas or additional clauses. Ask them to create their own sentence frames for different stages of the writing process. Have them lead peer review discussions using the frames.
